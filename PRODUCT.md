# PRODUCT.md — Postilize

register: brand

## Product purpose
Postilize is business-of-law AI for law firms. While practice-of-law AI (Harvey, Legora) compresses the hours inside legal work, Postilize creates work: it finds client needs before they become RFPs (Proactive Signals), expands existing client relationships (Proactive ERM), and keeps the firm's relationship data trustworthy (Proactive Cleanse). Backed by Litera.

## Users
Managing partners, CMOs, and business development leaders at mid-to-large law firms (AmLaw 200, international firms). Sophisticated, time-poor buyers who also evaluate Harvey and Legora; they equate visual restraint with institutional credibility.

## Brand voice
Assured, precise, quietly expensive. Like a managing partner's stationery: nothing decorative, everything deliberate. Short declarative sentences. No hype words, no exclamation marks, no em dashes.

## Strategic principles
- Say the argument once, well: AI shrinks matters → rates only buy time → originate more, earlier.
- Credibility early: firm logos and the Litera backing sit directly under the hero.
- Restraint is the differentiator. Every section earns its place; cut anything that restates.

## Anti-references
- The current postilize.com: 16 dense sections, mixed alignment, fake dashboard data, 10 testimonials, SaaS-blue gradients.
- Generic SaaS landing pages: icon-card grids, hero-metric templates, gradient text.

## References
- harvey.ai, legora.com: warm paper palette, editorial serif display, enormous whitespace.
- Smythson / legal stationery: the deep-green commitment.

## Constraints (this engagement)
- Single standalone index.html, embedded CSS, Google Fonts, no build step.
- All imagery is gray placeholder boxes with small labels naming what belongs there (explicit user request; real screenshots/photography come later).
